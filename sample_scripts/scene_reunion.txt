TITLE: "The Last Message"

INT. COFFEE SHOP - MORNING

Sarah sits at a corner table, nursing cold coffee. Her phone buzzes. She stares at the notification without opening it.

BARISTA
(calling out)
Sarah! Order for Sarah!

She ignores it. Instead, pulls out a worn envelope from her jacket pocket. Opens it with trembling hands. A letter. She reads it silently, tears forming.

The phone buzzes again. And again.

Finally, she looks. Twenty-three unread messages. All from MARCUS, the name that makes her chest tighten.

She starts typing a response. Deletes it. Types again. Deletes again.

SARAH
(to herself)
How do I explain?

A tall man enters the coffee shop. MARCUS. He hasn't seen her yet. Sarah's entire body goes rigid.

Marcus scans the room. Their eyes meet across the crowded coffee shop.

He walks toward her table. Each step feels eternal.

MARCUS
(standing over her)
I got your letter.

SARAH
(quietly)
I wasn't sure you would care.

MARCUS
Sarah... I've been calling for three days.

She holds up the envelope—his old letter, the one she's been carrying for two years.

SARAH
You said you didn't want to see me again. You were very clear.

MARCUS
I was angry. I was wrong.

Before he can sit, Sarah stands up. She's crying now, but her voice is steady.

SARAH (CONT'D)
It's too late, Marcus. I finally let go.

She walks toward the door. Marcus doesn't follow. He stands there, watching her disappear.

He slowly sits at the table she just left. Picks up the envelope. Opens it with shaking hands.

Inside: his letter, covered in her handwritten notes in the margins. A thousand conversations only she was brave enough to have.

He reads one margin note over and over:

"I wish you could hear me."

FADE TO BLACK.

THE END
